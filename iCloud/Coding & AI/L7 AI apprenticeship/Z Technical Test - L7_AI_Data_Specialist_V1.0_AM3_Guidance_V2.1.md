Introduction

Assessment  method  3  for  the  BCS  level  7  AI  Data  Specialist  apprenticeship  requires  the
apprentice to complete a technical test, consisting of 4 long-response questions, based on a
given scenario.

The apprentice will be provided with a written scenario which will describe:

•  A problem or opportunity
•  Data sources
•  Organisational context

Using the information provided in the scenario, candidates should propose a solution through
written responses to 4 questions.

Assessment plan criteria and syllabus

Apprentices must thoroughly study the assessment plan criteria and BCS syllabus related to
the  technical  test,  in  advance  of  the  assessment.  These  list  the  criteria  that  must  be
evidenced in the apprentice’s written answers (see the grading statements provided in the
‘Marking Guidance’ section of this document and the assessment plan).

The BCS syllabus is based on the assessment plan, and it provides more granular detail on
the content that apprentices may encounter in their technical test. The indicative content and
guidance featured in the syllabus serve as a guide to help apprentices plan their approach to
the  test  questions  and  consider  the  specific  techniques,  methods  and  tools  they  will
reference.

The  scenario  will  be  provided  at  the  beginning  of  the  assessment  by  the  independent
assessor and will be available throughout the assessment.

The questions relating to the solution proposed by the apprentice will be centred around the
pass criteria set out in the assessment plan. See pages 18-19 of the assessment plan for the
pass and distinction criteria, and page 28 for the underlying knowledge and skills criteria. For
example:

Pass criteria:

•  Differentiates  between  the  types  of  uncertainty  associated  with  the  outputs  of  data

collection and analysis.

A relating ‘skills’ criteria:

•  S21: Identify and quantify different kinds of uncertainty in the outputs of data collection,

experiments and analyses.

Structuring the answers

A time limit of 100 minutes applies to this assessment method, which includes reading time
and time to check the completed work.

A suggested breakdown for using this time is:

• 15 minutes to read the scenario and questions
• 15-20 minutes to compose each long response answer
• 5 minutes to proofread the completed work

In  the  test,  apprentices  shall  encounter  a  range  of  command  verbs.  These  descriptions
explain what each verb is asking the apprentice to do.
