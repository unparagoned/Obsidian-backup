## Overview of the Disruptive Leadership for Sustainable Strategy Practice Technical Test

Advances in automation, data availability, and artificial intelligence are increasingly shaping how organisations address complex business challenges. As organisations adopt digital and AI enabled solutions, there is a growing need for professionals who can design, justify, and evaluate technically sound data driven solutions that align with organisational objectives and constraints.

This practice technical test is designed to prepare you for the End Point Assessment technical test by simulating the type of scenario and questioning you will encounter. You will be presented with a fictional organisational scenario that requires you to propose an AI focused solution to a defined problem. Your responses should demonstrate your ability to apply data science and AI principles in a structured, practical, and business focused way.

Using the knowledge and skills developed throughout this module, you are expected to show how you would identify appropriate data sources, manage and analyse data, select and justify suitable machine learning approaches, and consider legal, ethical, and organisational factors that may affect implementation. The emphasis of this assessment is on technical reasoning, decision making, and your ability to explain how an AI solution could be developed and deployed within a real-world organisational context.

This activity mirrors the structure and expectations of the EPA technical test. While the scenario in the real assessment will be different, the nature of the questions and the competencies being assessed will be the same or very similar. The practice test therefore provides an opportunity to rehearse your approach, structure your answers effectively, and ensure you can clearly evidence the relevant knowledge, skills, and behaviours under test conditions.

**The assignment consists of one task:**

- A practice technical test centred around an AI-focussed solution development in an organisation.
    

### Practice Technical Test

You will be provided with a scenario which will include the requirement for you to specify an AI solution to a given challenge for an organisation. You will read the scenario and then answer the following questions about it. This task mirrors the task you will be faced with in the EPA Technical Test. The scenario in the real test will be different, but the questions will likely be the same or very similar.

Word count: The word recommendation for this section is up to 3,000 words (between 500 to 750 words per question).

Time allowed: In the EPA, you would normally be given no more than 100 minutes to complete this task, so you should aim to work within that timescale.

You can find out more about how the actual technical test is graded in the [End-point assessment plan for Artificial Intelligence (AI) Data Specialist apprenticeship standard.](https://skillsengland.education.gov.uk/media/3685/st0763_artificial-intelligence-ai-data-specialist_l7_ap-for-publication_qm.pdf "End-point assessment plan for Artificial Intelligence (AI) Data Specialist apprenticeship standard.")

**Section A: Core Knowledge (Short Answers)**

**Answer concisely, showing your reasoning.**

1. **Machine Learning Selection:**

Given a customer churn dataset, justify when to use a supervised vs. unsupervised learning method. Name

suitable algorithms and why.

2. **Data Cleaning Task:**

Describe steps to cleanse and validate a real-world transactional dataset with missing, duplicated, and

outlier values. Why are each step and control necessary in a regulated business context ?

3. **Model Evaluation:**

Choose two different model performance metrics for a classification task. For each, explain what it

measures, when it is most appropriate, and why you might supplement it with others.

4. **Bias & Fairness:**

Describe a practical method to detect and mitigate algorithmic bias in an HR CV-screening AI tool.

Provide at least one real mitigation action.

**Section B: Practical Coding/Design (Applied Response)**

**Write code snippets or structured pseudocode as needed.**

5. **Data Pipeline Design:**

Outline a high-level data pipeline for a predictive maintenance AI, from raw machine sensor data to

actionable insight. Include quality and compliance checks.

6. **Feature Engineering Example:**

Take a time-series dataset (e.g., retail sales). Give an example of one synthetic feature, and explain why it

adds business value.

7. **Algorithm Choice & Justification:**

Given prediction accuracy targets and constraints on interpretability, select between a decision tree,

random forest, and neural network for credit risk scoring. Justify your choice for a regulated lender.**Section C: Analysis & Communication (Written/Diagram)**

8. **Alternatives Appraisal:**

Compare two possible approaches (e.g., rules-based vs. machine learning solution) for fraud detection.

Use a weighted decision matrix to recommend one, with justification.

9. **Result Communication Scenario:**

Draft a short executive summary (150 words) explaining the impact of model performance, business

value, and risks to a non-technical board member.

**Technical Test Guidance**

**Preparation**

• Master core techniques: supervised/unsupervised ML, data analysis, basic stats, model assessment, bias

handling.

• Review how to design and comment data processing pipelines, including regulatory controls.

• Know how to justify tool, language, and algorithm choices, balancing accuracy, interpretability, cost, and

compliance.

• Be ready to evidence alternative solutions, metrics, impact, and to communicate for both technical and

business audiences.

**During the Test**

• Read all requirements closely: note context (business, compliance, user impact).

• Approach each answer with—criteria → alternative options → justification → impact/limitations.

• Prioritise clarity: use concise comments in code, labelled diagrams, and bullet points.

• For written responses, always cover: value, risks, mitigation, and how the answer fits business priorities.

• Where possible, include a brief confounding factors or limitations section ("If X data changes…")

• When asked to communicate results, use plain English, illustrate with a key stat or chart, and reference

business outcomes.

**Distinction Behaviours**

• Show commercial and ethical awareness—discuss constraints, risks, and explain your trade-offs.• Critically evaluate: don’t just state what you did; analyse why, what alternatives were rejected, and why

your approach fits the problem.

• Demonstrate ability to adapt explanations for diverse audiences (board vs. engineers vs. users).

• Reference real professional practice and evidence wherever possible.

For best results, simulate real technical assessment conditions: set time limits, answer unseen variants of these

questions, and self-evaluate your answers by checking if they cover justification, impact, and clear

communication—as outlined above.
