Work-based Project Report & Questions Guidance

Overview:

The Project Report is a formal 5,000-word (+10%) write-up of a real work-based AI/data science project,

followed by questioning. This tests your practical skills in delivering, evidencing, and defending an end-to-end

solution aligned to business value and organisational priorities.

Structure & Content

1.

Introduction/Background: Context on organisation, challenge, and business priority.

2. Business Problem & Value: Deﬁne problem, state why and how it matters, quantify impact if solved.

3. Methods/Justiﬁcation: List approaches (methodologies, tools, programming), and justify your choices

against alternatives.

4. Scope & KPIs: Deﬁne boundaries, measurable indicators of success.

5. Data Handling: Detail selection, cleaning, validation, ethical controls, and data governance.

6. Alternatives Survey: Compare approaches (pros/cons, trade-offs, rationale).

7.

Implementation: Explain pipelines, technical setup, evaluation metrics, error/bias controls.

8. Results: Present ﬁndings, link to business goals, and critically reﬂect.

9. Discussion/Recommendations: Analyse outcomes, propose improvements, lessons learned, and potential

wider impact.

10. Summary/Implications: Highlight main takeaways and strategic actions.

11. Caveats/Limitations: List uncertainties and how different decisions could affect results.

12. Appendices: Include artefacts—code, ﬁgures, tables, KSB mapping, employer sign-off, evidence packs.

Questioning Guidance

•

Prepare to answer on business need, method/technique selection, trade-offs, bias/error mitigation, value

evidence, stakeholder engagement, communication adaptation, and commercial prioritisation.

• Use critical reasoning: Always frame answers by criteria, alternative assessment, justiﬁcations, and

business impact.

• Cite project evidence: Name your artefacts (BRD, code, test logs, KPI dashboards, decision logs,

stakeholder feedback).

• Defend and evaluate: State what else you might have done, how you evaluated risks, and what you learned

for future business practice.

Distinction Behaviours

• Make commercial trade-offs explicit, defending choices via metrics/ROI.

• Critically evaluate methodologies, alternatives, limitations, and business implications.

• Communicate and justify solutions in executive, technical, and stakeholder-friendly formats.

•

Evidence impact on organisational priorities and future business practice.
