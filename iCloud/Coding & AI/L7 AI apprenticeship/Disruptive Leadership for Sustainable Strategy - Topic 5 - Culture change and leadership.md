# Main

Digital Transformation is the creation of a continuously learning and adapting AI-driven and human-empowered business model that seeks to identify, codify and operationalise actionable customer, product and operational insights in order to optimise operational efficiency, enhance customer value creation, migrate operational and compliance risk and create new revenue opportunities.

Eight laws of digital transformation
1. It's about business models, not just business processes
2. It's about digital transformation not digitalisation
3. It's about speaking the language of your customers.
4. It's about creating new digital assets
5. It's about transitioning from predicting to prescribing to autonomous
6. It's about AI-driven autonomous operations and policies
7. It's about identifying, codifying and operationalising source value.
8. It's about the three horizons of digital transformation

Three horizons of digital transformation

1. Optimise your operations - reduce compliance and management risks, new  revenue, better customer experience.
2. Digitize your operations - improve operational effectiveness and business model
3. Digitally transformed business - reinvent the organisation with continuous exploring, learning and adaption of the business model acting upon new customer, product and operation insights with minimal human intervention

Empower people
1. Make sure people understand mission statement
2. Speak the language of the customer, same goals, assets, etc.
3. Empower teams through flexibility.
4. Embrace an "AND" mentality. Create better produce and services without any compromise
5. Embrace critical thinking. 

Speaking the customer language is more than just language it's about putting yourself in their shoes.

Design thinking

- Empathising
- Defining or framing the customers challenge
- Ideating potential solutions
- Prototyping different solution options
- Testing, learning and refining

Leadership

According to McKinsey (2024):

> ‘Leadership is a set of mindsets and behaviours that aligns people in a collective direction, enables them to work together and accomplish shared goals, and helps them adjust to changing environments’.

- ‘Leadership is the energetic process of getting people fully and willingly committed to a new and sustainable course of action, to meet commonly agreed objectives whilst having commonly held values.’
    
- ‘Management is essentially and inherently a social and moral activity, in which success is achieved by building organisational patterns, culture, and understandings based on relationships of mutual trust and shared obligation among people involved with the organisation.’
