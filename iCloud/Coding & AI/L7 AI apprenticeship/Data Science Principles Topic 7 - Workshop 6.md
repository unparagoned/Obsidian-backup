What should be contained in a data governance policy

- Purpose and Scope:  
    Explain the policy’s objectives, the stakeholders involved, and which data assets and systems are covered.
- Data Ownership and Accountability:  
    Define roles and responsibilities such as data stewards, custodians, and users, ensuring clear accountability for data quality and protection.
- Data Classification:  
    Establish a framework for classifying data (e.g., public, internal, confidential, sensitive) based on its value and risk.
- Data Quality Management:  
    Outline processes and standards for ensuring data accuracy, consistency, and reliability throughout its lifecycle.
- Data Security and Privacy Controls:  
    Detail the measures to protect data against unauthorized access and breaches, including encryption, access controls, and compliance with privacy regulations.
- Data Lifecycle Management:  
    Describe procedures for data creation, storage, usage, archiving, and destruction, ensuring data is managed throughout its useful life.
- Compliance and Regulatory Requirements:  
    Incorporate guidelines to ensure adherence to industry standards, legal requirements, and internal policies.
- Audit, Monitoring, and Reporting:  
    Define mechanisms for regular auditing and monitoring of data practices, and establish reporting protocols for data-related issues or breaches.
- Training and Awareness:  
    Include strategies for educating staff on data governance policies and best practices.
- Policy Review and Updates:  
    Establish a process for regular review and revision of the policy to ensure it remains effective in a changing environment.

- Clear policies and standards are essential for defining how data should be managed and used. This includes data privacy policies, data quality standards, and guidelines for data storage and access.
- Data Stewards are responsible for overseeing data governance activities. They ensure that data policies and standards are implemented and maintained, and they act as custodians of data quality and compliance.
- Ensuring data quality is a core component of data governance. This involves processes for validating data accuracy, completeness, consistency, and reliability.
- Protecting data from unauthorised access and ensuring compliance with data protection regulations, such as GDPR, is crucial. This includes implementing security measures and managing data access controls.
- Managing data throughout its lifecycle, from creation to deletion, ensures that data remains relevant and useful. This involves defining data retention policies and procedures for data archiving and disposal.
- Metadata provides context for data, describing its source, structure, and usage. Effective metadata management enhances data discoverability and usability.
- Establishing a governance council or committee comprising key stakeholders ensures that data governance activities align with organisational goals and receive appropriate oversight.
- Defining performance metrics to measure the effectiveness of data governance initiatives and regularly monitoring these metrics ensures continuous improvement.
