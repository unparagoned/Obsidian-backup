Overview
This document provides information about the requirements and format of assessment
method 1 (AM1), which comprises a project report and presentation with supplementary
questioning. This is part of your end-point assessment (EPA).
Apprentices undertake AM1, assessment method 2 (AM2), and assessment method 3
(AM3) following entry through the gateway. These assessment methods can be undertaken
in any order. Each KSB is assessed by one assessment method only.
AI data specialists investigate and develop AI solutions fuelled by data, aiming to
streamline and enhance business operations through automation. Their goal is to assist,
empower, and improve human decision-making processes.
AM1 of this apprenticeship qualification requires apprentices to produce a project report,
based on a pre-gateway work-based project. The report will be the basis of a presentation
to the independent assessor, followed by supplementary questioning immediately after the
presentation. The apprentice will compile the project report after they have gone through
the gateway process.
The project report enables the apprentice to demonstrate a broad set of knowledge, skills
and behaviours (KSBs) related to this occupation. It can produce something which is of
genuine business benefit to the employer.

Component 1 – Project report
A project brief must be submitted to the EPAO at the gateway. The EPAO will provide the
title of the report within 2 weeks of receiving the project brief which must scope out the
work-based project and should include a summary of the stages covered by the work-
based project and an overview of the tasks as well as the specific responsibilities and
duties assigned and undertaken by the apprentice.
Apprentices must submit their project report to the EPAO by the end of week 6 after the
EPAO has agreed the project title, and at least one week before the presentation.
The project should allow for the KSBs relevant to the EPA to be demonstrated (as detailed
under the ‘Assessment grading’ section of this guide).
The employer should ensure that the project has a real business application and will ensure
that it meets the requirements of the EPA (including suitable coverage of the KSBs
assigned to this assessment method).

Choosing a project topic
The apprentice and employer should peruse the project titles and related specifications

apprentice and employer should carefully consider what project topic relates most closely
to the role of the apprentice during the EPA period.

The work-based project may address any of the following issues:
• An idea/opportunity to use AI or new developments in the AI/Machine Learning field
in the business
• A specific business problem to be addressed using AI
• A recurring issue.
Typical project titles could include:
• Usage of AI to optimise processes or allocation of resources
• How AI may be used to improve customer service
• Use of AI to predict demand and changing behaviours.
The project report must be based on the pre-gateway real work-based project carried out in
the employer’s workplace as part of the apprentice’s day-to-day activities. The above list is
not exhaustive, and apprentices and employer should consider what relevant work-based
project are most suitable for demonstrating the required KSBs.
What should the project report include?
As a minimum, all project outputs must include:
• Introduction and background.
• Outline of the issue or opportunity and the business problem to be solved.
• Methods used and justification.
• The scope of the project (including key performance indicators).
• Data selection, collection and pre-processing.
• Survey of potential alternatives.
• Implementation - performance metrics.
• Results.
• Discussion and conclusions/recommendations.
• Summary of findings.
• Implications.
• Caveats and limitations.

The apprentice will need to consider the availability of company and external resources
required to complete the project. They must also ensure they are fully aware of the KSBs
the project is intended to assess as that is what the grading of the project will be based on.
The apprentice may work as part of a team which could include technical internal or
external support however the report will be the apprentice’s own work and will be reflective
of their own role and contribution.
When the project is submitted, the employer and the apprentice should verify the submitted
work is that of the apprentice.
Word count
The word limit for the project report is specified as 5000 words with a tolerance of plus or
minus 10%. Please note, if the word limit is exceeded, the project report may be rejected
and the EPA cannot progress.
Apprentices are asked to include all content they wish to be assessed in the main body of
the project. Note that appendices, references, diagrams, etc. are not included in the overall
word count total. Apprentices are advised that the appendices may not be read in full so
should not be used for presenting new information, however, they will be referred to as
specified within the main project text.
Component 2 – Presentation with supplementary questioning
The apprentice will be required to prepare a 30-minute presentation that will be delivered
within a suitably controlled environment, typically online.
The presentation will be followed by a question-and-answer session which will typically last
for 45 minutes, making the overall duration of this assessment 75 minutes. The
independent assessor can increase the time of the presentation and questioning by up to
10%, to allow the apprentice to complete their last answer.

What should the presentation cover?
The presentation will cover the KSBs assigned to this method of assessment as shown in
the ‘How is this assessment graded?’ section of this guide.
The presentation will be based on the project report and will cover:
• A high-level summary of the main aspects of the project report.
• Context/implications/recommendations from the project report.
• Research undertaken.
• Practical application of knowledge, skills and behaviours to complete the project
report.
• Business recommendations.
• Any follow-on outcomes.
• Actions and next steps.
Delivering the presentation
The apprentice will submit the presentation to BCS within 8 weeks of the project title being
agreed with the EPAO. The presentation will take place on a one-to-one basis with an
independent assessor assigned to the apprentice. The apprentice will be given 2 weeks’
notice of the presentation date to prepare for the presentation.
To deliver the presentation, the apprentice will have access to:
• Flip chart
• Work products
• Notes
• Interactive boards
• PowerPoint
• Videos
• Computer
• Interactive demonstrations
As this is an open-book session, apprentices are permitted and encouraged to use their
presentation notes during the presentation and questioning. A minimum of 10 questions will
be asked at the end of the presentation. Follow-up questions are also allowed and do not
form part of the question number count.

The presentation and questioning elements must be conducted in a suitably controlled
environment in any of the following:
• the employer’s premises
• a suitable venue selected by the EPAO (for example a training provider’s premises)
EPAOs must ensure appropriate methods to prevent misrepresentation are in place when
using remote assessment tools.
How will the apprentice’s work be submitted?
Evidence is an integral part of AM1, and the independent assessor will use the project
submitted to BCS to familiarise themselves with the apprentice’s work prior to the
presentation with questioning.
Apprentices must be given both written and verbal instructions for the task they must
complete, including the timescales they are working to.
The apprentice will submit:
• A 5000-word project report in an electronic format.
• A copy of the presentation, designed to last 30 minutes.
Both items will need to be submitted by the employer and apprentice to the EPAO. When
they are ready for submission, the training provider will assist the employer and apprentice
in submitting the documents via the ACE apprenticeship management system. The
documents are then allocated to an independent assessor by BCS, so there is no direct
contact between the employer or apprentice and the independent assessor until the
apprentice and assessor meet at the assessment meeting.
