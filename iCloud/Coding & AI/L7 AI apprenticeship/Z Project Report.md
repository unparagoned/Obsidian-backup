
# Instructions

**Assessment Method 1: Project evaluation report with presentation and questions**

This Assessment Method has two components: You will complete a 5,000-word project evaluation report, which will be followed by a presentation and supplementary questioning session with an independent assessor from the EPAO.

Before you complete the project report, you will submit a project brief with information about the project you intend to use for the assessment. This will be signed off by the EPAO to ensure that it will offer you the opportunity to demonstrate the KSBs that are mapped to this Assessment Method.

**Assessment Method 2: Professional discussion** 

For the second part of the EPA, you will take part in a professional discussion with an independent assessor. This will be appropriately structured to draw out the best of your competence and excellence and will cover the KSBs assigned for this Assessment Method.  

**Assessment Method 3: Technical test**

The technical test, either computer- or paper-based, consists of four scenario-based questions, is closed-book, lasts up to 100 minutes, and allows the use of a calculator, pen, and notepaper. The technical test covers the KSBs for this Assessment Method.

## Overview of Assessment Method 1

This Assessment Method has got two components. An overview of each is provided below.

1. Project evaluation report (5,000 words +/- 10%)
    
2. Presentation with supplementary questions
    

You will complete a 5,000-word (+/- 10%) project evaluation report based on a work-based project that you have completed whilst you have been on programme. At **Gateway,** you will submit your project brief to the EPAO who will sign it off and provide you with a project title. In order to complete this, you will need to download your [project brief template](https://app.qa.com/download/dam/cce6eb41-75fb-4c78-8610-cfd712ca5ae2 "project brief template").

Once the EPAO has signed off your project brief, you will have at least **six weeks** to submit your project evaluation report.

The project evaluation report must be based on the work-based project that you carried out in your workplace as part of your day-to-day activities.

**What should the project be about?**

Your work-based project is completed during the programme and may address any of the following issues:

- An idea/opportunity to use AI or new developments in the AI/machine learning field in the business. 
    
- A specific business problem to be addressed using AI.
    
- A recurring issue. 
    

You may have worked as part of a team on the project, which could include technical internal or external support. However, the report will be your own work and will reflect your own role and contribution.

Typical project titles could include:

- Usage of AI to optimise processes or allocation of resources 
    
- How AI may be used to improve customer service 
    
- Use of AI to predict demand and changing behaviours 
    

At Gateway, you will submit your project brief for the EPAO to sign off.  

In completing the project, report, and presentation, you will demonstrate that you can apply the [KSBs that are designated for this Assessment Method in a real-life working environment](https://app.qa.com/download/dam/49021df0-190a-4650-b678-b82a0b0a5056).



In this part of Assessment Method 1, you will complete a project report, comprising of a written report with a word count of 5,000 words. A tolerance of plus or minus 10% is allowed. Appendices, references, diagrams, and tables will not be included in this total. 

The format of the project report should be as follows: 

- Introduction and background 
    
- Outline of the issue or opportunity and the business problem to be solved 
    
- Methods used and justification 
    
- The scope of the project (including key performance indicators)  
    
- Data selection, collection, and pre-processing 
    
- Survey of potential alternatives 
    
- Implementation – performance metrics 
    
- Results 
    
- Discussion and conclusions/recommendations 
    
- Summary of findings 
    
- Implications 
    
- Caveats and limitations 
    
- Appendices: 
    
    - Code and documentation used for the project, e.g., coding developed. 
        
    - Statistical rigour – To include uncertainty/bias/error estimates as appropriate. 
        
    - Figures/tables/visualisation as appropriate to project.
        
    - Mapping of the project report to the KSBs for this Assessment Method.
        
    - Verification by the apprentice's employer that the project report is a true reflection of the apprentice's involvement, and the report is their own work. 
        

You can find more information and guidance on the project and presentation in the [AM1 Project and Presentation Guidance for Employers and Candidates](https://app.qa.com/download/dam/2dba8938-988a-461c-960e-474e591d47aa "AM1 Project and Presentation Guidance for Employers and Candidates")document.


**Component 2: Presentation with questions**

In the second part, you will deliver a presentation based on your written project report to an independent assessor.

Your presentation must cover:

- a high-level summary of the main aspects of the project report.
    
- context/implications/recommendations from the project report.
    
- details of the research undertaken.
    
- how you have applied application of knowledge, skills and behaviours in practice to complete the project report.
    
- your business recommendations.
    
- any follow-on outcomes.
    
- actions and next steps.

You will complete and submit the presentation after the gateway and following the submission of your project report, which you will then present to an independent assessor, either face-to-face or via online video conferencing. If using an online platform, EPAOs must ensure appropriate measures are in place to prevent misrepresentation.

The presentation with questions lasts around 75 minutes. This will typically include a presentation of 30 minutes and questioning lasting 45 minutes. The independent assessor must use the full time available for questioning. They can increase the time of the presentation and questioning by up to 10%. This time is to allow you to complete your last point or respond to a question if necessary.  

The independent assessor must ask at least 10 questions. Follow-up questions are allowed where clarification is required.  

Next up, you’ll get an overview of the KSBs and how you can achieve a pass or distinction for Assessment Method 1.
