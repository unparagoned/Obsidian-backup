## Cybercrime

Stole $10m from US bank. 1994

Phishing - fake emails

Identity theft - misusing data

Hacking - Misusing and shutting down sites or systems

Ransomware: block access to a system until money is paid

Solutions

Strong passwords

Updating software

Think before clicking,

Multifactor authentication

### Preventing ransomware

Don't click unverified links

Scan email

Firewalls and endpoint protection

Only download from trusted sites

Keep backup of data

Use VPN when using public wi-fi

Use security software

Don't use unfamiliar usb devices

Avoid giving out personal data

Zero trust model - even if on network require additional security

## Society media safety

[https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/online-safety-act-explainer/online-safety-act-explainer#:~:text=The%20Online%20Safety%20Act%202023,users%27%20safety%20on%20their%20platforms](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/online-safety-act-explainer/online-safety-act-explainer#:~:text=The%20Online%20Safety%20Act%202023,users%27%20safety%20on%20their%20platforms).

UK online safety acts tries to limit danger

- Remove illegal content quickly or prevent it from appearing in the first place
- Prevent children from accessing harmful or age inappropriate content
- Enforce age limits
- Ensure risks and dangers to children are more transparent and publish risk assessments
- Provide parents and children clear and accessible ways to report problems online.

## Carbon footprint and AI

Data centres use lots of electricity, water for cooling and other equipment.

Training a single LLM can emit more than 626,000 lbs of CO2. Nearly five times the emissions of a car over its lifetime.

There is e-waste.

## Accessibility and AI

- Visual assistance from AI
- AI for live transcription
- AI for mobility issues. AI with cameras can help people with visual impairment. They can navigate the real world.

AI could identify people with disabilities and treat them differently which could be a risk.
