Professional Discussion Guidance

Overview:

The Professional Discussion is a structured interview, probing your applied AI & data science knowledge,

decision-making, commercial thinking, leadership, ethics, and technical practice against industry KSBs. Prepare

to reference real projects, justify techniques, and demonstrate the impact both technically and commercially.

How to Prepare

• Know the KSBs and themes: Review example questions mapped to themes (statistical foundations,

engineering, testing, mathematical reasoning, stakeholder inﬂuence, ethics, professional practice).

• Build your evidence bank: For each KSB/theme, list 1-2 examples from your own work; focus on

decisions made, reasoning, alternative approaches, commercial/trade-offs, and measurable impact.

•

Practice with the list: Simulate answering in STAR format (Situation, Task, Action, Result), critically

evaluating your decisions and their business impact.

• Anticipate adaptation: Be ready to explain challenges, lessons learned, and how your approach evolved

to suit different technical, business, and regulatory contexts.

Answering Approach

• Be concise but complete: Answer directly, covering why, how, impact, adaptation, and evidence.

•

Justify choices: Always explain your rationale, why other options were rejected, and show commercial

awareness.

• Reference evidence: Use clear, speciﬁc artefacts—code, dashboards, business cases, test logs, stakeholder

feedback.

• Demonstrate breadth: Touch technical detail (algorithms, data pipelines, metrics) and wider implications

(compliance, ethics, leadership, communication).

•

Show adaptability: Explain how you communicated or worked differently for mixed audiences, and cite

the measurable results.

• Methodological Approach: Try to use STARR or a similar methodology to keep track of your answer

and to stay consistent

Distinction Behaviours

• Critically assess and adapt practice.

• Clearly evidence inﬂuence on working practices, business decisions, and solution quality.

• Articulate the commercial and ethical trade-offs made—and justify them in context.
