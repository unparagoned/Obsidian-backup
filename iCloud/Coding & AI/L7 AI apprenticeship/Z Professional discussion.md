
# Instructions 

The professional discussion will last for 60 minutes. The assessor may extend the session by 10% to allow you to complete your final discussion point. Ideally, the professional discussion should take place in a quiet room, free from distractions and influence (such as the employer’s premises or another suitable venue such as a training provider).

**Remember to address the KSBs** 

- Remember, the Knowledge, Skills, and Behaviours (KSBs) are what you are being assessed against! In order to get a higher grade, you will need to purposely target and cover the distinction criteria as well as covering all of the pass criteria. 
    
- As part of your journal that you will submit as part of this assessment method, you will need to include a supporting section on mapping to state where in your work you have addressed each KSB. We recommend that you consistently monitor this to avoid accidentally missing criteria or trying to rush additional content to meet the requirements last-minute. 
    
- You can use the [Portfolio Checklist](https://app.qa.com/download/dam/bf676f4f-bf70-4af5-bcb7-f7983cab4554 "Portfolio Checklist") to make this easier. We suggest you download this now if you haven’t already.


**Rehearse for your professional discussion:** 

- Before your professional discussion, make sure you practise to help refine your timings and perfect your style. You could ask your team, a friend, or a family member to listen and give you feedback. Or consider filming yourself answering sample questions on your phone or computer. You could even just practise in a mirror! 
    
- It doesn’t have to be on your journal – you can practise discussing any subject you know well.
    
- Discussions are hard when you don’t know the content. But keep in mind that your professional discussion is focused on the work you’ve completed, researched, and analysed for over 15 months. You therefore know the subject like the back of your hand and can be confident in speaking about your own experiences and findings. 
    
- You can use the KSBs to practise your professional discussion. 
    
- If you find you are struggling to recall everything you want to say, consider using presenter notes or cue cards to help you when answering questions. 
    
- This is **your** professional discussion about **you** and the tasks you have carried out in your role. Try to avoid talking about 'we' when you discuss work-based activities.

## Professional discussion – what can I expect?

- Reference portfolio
- Replay the question
- Use language from KSBs
- State your roles and responsibilities
- Use notes
- Speak in first person
- Show your knowledge
- Use STARR
- Mentions various techniques like SWAT analysis or PESSEL? analysis.

Be Familiar with KSBs.

**The purpose of the professional discussion is to:**

1. confirm and validate judgments about your KSBs.
    
2. explore aspects of the work, including how it was carried out, in more detail.
    
3. provide further evidence for the assessor to make a holistic decision about the professional discussion grade to be awarded.
    

The professional discussion can draw on broader experience from the workplace, but the initial and primary focus will be to discuss and showcase your depth of understanding around the KSBs that may not naturally occur as part of the project.

The professional discussion will be undertaken by an EPA assessor, who will make the grading decision.

It is only after all three Assessment Methods have been completed that the final grading decision can be made. 

**Tips**  

1. Think about your apprenticeship journey. How have you identified areas you wanted to develop, and how have you developed these during your time as an apprentice?  
    
2. Highlight the key things you have learnt in relation to the assessed KSBs. Consider how you have applied this knowledge and what impact this has made at work. 
    
3. Write down two or three key points which link to each area of the KSBs assessed by the professional discussion, including the distinction criteria. 
    
4. Focus on what you have created/designed during your apprenticeship. What have you achieved?
    
5. What did you find easy/hard about the tasks/projects?
    
6. How did you resolve any issues?  
    
7. What would you do differently and why?  
    
8. How did they relate to your behaviours?
    

**Journal evidence – Triangulation** 

We strongly advise you to create a [journal](https://app.qa.com/download/dam/d72b6e39-1b12-4cdb-9e9d-68bb5befc563) evidencing the relevant KSBs you developed while in the programme. The journal will prepare you for the professional discussion with the independent assessor. 

To evidence all KSB criteria in your journal, it is recommended that you triangulate your evidence by (as shown in Figure 1): 

1. Write a **narrative** about a work activity you have completed in your role. It might help to use the **STAR** technique (**S:** what was the **situation**, **T:** what did you have to do?, **A:** what **actions** did you take?, **R:** what were the **results?**).
    
2. Provide a **witness testimony** or **observation** to confirm your performance.
    
3. Back it up with some work **product evidence**, such as meeting minutes, documentation, or reports.
    

Triangulation means that your evidence should be **contextualised** and that you should have reflected on it.

To provide **context**, make sure your triangulated evidence is placed within its theoretical context to confirm your knowledge. This should include research.

In your **reflection**, you should answer the following:

- What did you learn from this?
    
- How could you improve?
    
- Will you use the same practice again? If yes/no, why?
    
- Is there anything you wish to see implemented since this situation? What was it and why should the process be implemented in the future?
    

How does this compare with what you have learnt from the workshops and other methodologies?
