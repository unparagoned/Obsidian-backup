# Generic Exam Guide for All Learners

This section provides a universal framework and tips for approaching the BCS Level 7 AI Technical Test.

## The DELTA Framework

Use this structured approach for every substantive question. It ensures you cover what assessors are looking for at Level 7.

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Step** | **What to Include** |
| **D - Define** | Start with a clear technical definition. What is the concept? How does it work? Use precise terminology. |
| **E - Evidence** | Ground your answer in the case study. Provide concrete examples, specific data sources, or real scenarios from the brief. |
| **L - Link** | Connect to business impact. What does this mean for cost, delivery speed, operational change, or strategic objectives? |
| **T - Trade-offs** | Compare at least two approaches. State pros/cons for each. Justify why your chosen approach is most suitable for this context. |
| **A - Acknowledge** | State limitations, risks, and uncertainty. How confident are you? What could go wrong? What assumptions are you making? |

## Applying DELTA: Question-by-Question

**Data Questions (Extraction, Linking, Uncertainty)**

* **Define:** State what uncertainty means technically (aleatory vs epistemic, measurement error vs model error)
* **Evidence:** Name specific datasets from the case study and explain extraction methods
* **Link:** Explain how data quality affects business outcomes
* **Trade-offs:** Compare data sources or integration approaches
* **Acknowledge:** State confidence levels, ranges, or what assumptions you are making

**Technical/ML Questions (Storage, Algorithms, Architecture)**

* **Define:** Explain the technical concept (e.g., what gradient boosting does, how a data lake differs from a warehouse)
* **Evidence:** Apply to the case study with specific requirements
* **Link:** Justify why this supports the business objective
* **Trade-offs:** Compare at least two algorithms/approaches with pros and cons
* **Acknowledge:** Note model limitations, scalability concerns, or maintenance requirements

**Business/Ethics Questions (Constraints, Impact, Compliance)**

* **Define:** Name the specific regulation, ethical principle, or business constraint
* **Evidence:** Show how it applies to the case study context
* **Link:** Quantify impact: cost, time, resource, reputational
* **Trade-offs:** Weigh compliance burden against risk of non-compliance
* **Acknowledge:** Note evolving regulations or areas of ambiguity

**Delivery/Architecture Questions (Design, Deploy, Resources)**

* **Define:** State your methodology (Agile, waterfall, hybrid) and why
* **Evidence:** Walk through the end-to-end pipeline with case-specific details
* **Link:** Connect architecture decisions to operational needs
* **Trade-offs:** Compare at least one alternative methodology and explain why you rejected it
* **Acknowledge:** Include governance, monitoring, and rollback plans

## Quick Reference: Exam Tips

|  |  |
| --- | --- |
| **Pitfall** | **What To Do Instead** |
| Vague on uncertainty | Give a technical definition. Mention confidence intervals, prediction ranges, or probability distributions. |
| Abrupt endings | Use signposting: "Having covered X, I now turn to Y..." and conclude each section. |
| Shallow business impact | Always ask: "What does this mean for cost, speed, or organisational change?" |
| No alternatives | Mention at least one rejected option with a reason. Use a quick pros/cons table if time allows. |
| Generic answers | Name systems, datasets, and stakeholders from the case study. Make it specific. |

## Time Management Strategy

The technical test typically has 4 main questions. Allocate time proportionally to marks, but ensure you cover all parts:

1. Read the entire case study first (5-10 minutes)
2. Plan your DELTA structure for each question (2-3 minutes per question)
3. Write your answers, checking off each DELTA element
4. Reserve 5 minutes at the end to add signposting and check completeness

## Final Checklist Before Submitting

* Every technical concept has a clear definition
* I have referenced specific elements from the case study
* Business impact is quantified or evaluated (not just mentioned)
* At least one alternative approach is discussed with justification
* Uncertainty/limitations are acknowledged
* Sections have clear transitions and conclusions
